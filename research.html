Spruce Budworm Detection on 1-meter Aerial Imagery

My capstone research project for my GIS graduate certificate investigated the potential for using Random Forest classification on 1-meter National Agricultural Imagery Program (NAIP) aerial photography to identify areas of tree defoliation due to spruce budworm.

Study Site

My study area is in the southern range of the white spruce in the boreal forest in northern Wisconsin, Michigan, and Minnesota.

Highlights

Workflow developed for NAIP(4-band) imagery to classify defoliation using machine learning (Random Forest) change analysis on individual tree scale (3m) to monitor defoliation by insect pests
